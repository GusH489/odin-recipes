How to make Yakiniku

Description

Yakiniku is a japanese dish that means "grilled meat" and is a vibrant and interactive dining experience that brings people together around the grill. Thin slices of marinated or plain meat are grilled at the table along with a colorful array of vegetables. Each bit is dipped in a rich, savory-sweet sauce (tare) made with soy sauce, mirin, garlic, and sesame oil, enhancing the umami flavors of the grilled ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 300 - 500 g thinly sliced beef (ribeye, short rib, or sirloin work best)
  • Assorted vegetables (sliced bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, zucchini, cabbage, or carrots)
  • 4 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp of sake (or dry wine)
  • 2 tbsp of mirin (or honey + water substitute)
  • 1 tbsp of sugar
  • 2 tsp of sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves (grated or minced)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1-2 green onions
  • Optional: 1 tsp gochujang (Korean chili paste) or chili flakes for spice

Steps:

  1. Make the sauce (tare):
    • In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, sake mirim and sugar.
    • Heat on low until sugar dissolves.
    • Remove from heat, stir in sesame oil, garlic, ginger, sesame seeds, and green onions
    • Let it cool.
  2. Prepare the grill/pan:
    • Traditionally, yakiniku is cooked oon a tabletop grill, but a cast-iron skillet or grill pan works well.
    • Heat until very hot.
  3. Cook the meat & veggies:
    • Lightly oil the grill/pan.
    • Grill the beef slices quickly (around 5-10 seconds per side).
    • Grill veggies until tender.
  4. Serve:
    • Dip the grilled meat and veggies in the tare sauce.
    • Enjoy with steamed rice, kimchi, or lettuce wraps.
